Disaster Awareness • Paghahanda sa Sakuna

Learn the major hazards affecting Camarines Norte, how they behave, and what you can do before, during, and after an event.

Typhoon • Bagyo

Wind & Rain Hazard

Camarines Norte is directly exposed to the Pacific, making it vulnerable to strong typhoons that bring destructive winds, heavy rain, coastal flooding, and storm surge. Coastal and low-lying barangays are most at risk.

Safety Tips:

  • Secure roofing, windows, and light materials around your house.
  • Charge phones, radios, and power banks before the storm hits.
  • Prepare food and potable water good for at least 3 days.
  • Evacuate early if advised by MDRRMO or barangay officials.
  • Stay updated via radio, official social media, and TV advisories.

Flooding • Baha

Water Hazard

Flooding occurs in low-lying and river-adjacent barangays such as Matnog, Mampili (Basud), Tigbinan & Fundado (Labo), and Gahonon & Lag-on (Daet). Sudden rise in water levels can trap residents if they delay evacuation.

Safety Tips:

  • Keep important documents and electronics in waterproof containers.
  • Never walk or drive through floodwaters; currents can be stronger than they look.
  • Turn off main electrical switches when water starts entering the house.
  • Move to higher floors or designated evacuation centers immediately.
  • Boil water or use purification tablets after floods to avoid disease.

Earthquake • Lindol

Ground Movement

Earthquakes can strike without warning. Even if Camarines Norte is not on a major fault line, shaking from nearby faults can still damage older structures and trigger landslides in mountainous barangays.

Safety Tips:

  • During shaking: DUCK, COVER, and HOLD under sturdy furniture.
  • Stay away from glass windows, tall shelves, and heavy hanging objects.
  • After the quake, move to open spaces and watch out for aftershocks.
  • Check for gas leaks, damaged electrical lines, and cracks in walls.
  • Have a go-bag ready near exits with essentials and first aid supplies.

Landslide • Pagguho ng Lupa

Soil Movement

Prolonged heavy rains soften and weaken soil, especially in sloped areas like Larap & Motherlode (Jose Panganiban), Itok & Catabaguangan (Capalonga), and upland sitios in Labo. Landslides can bury homes and block roads.

Safety Tips:

  • Watch for warning signs like cracks in the ground or leaning trees/posts.
  • Evacuate early if your home is near a steep slope or cliff.
  • Avoid sleeping in lower floors if your house is on a hillside.
  • Report unusual ground movement to barangay officials immediately.
  • Follow MDRRMO evacuation orders without delay.

Storm Surge • Daluyong

Coastal Hazard

Storm surge happens when strong winds push seawater inland, causing sea levels to rise rapidly. This can flood coastal houses, destroy roads, and carry debris far from the shoreline.

Safety Tips:

  • Evacuate to higher ground when storm surge warnings are raised.
  • Do not stay near the beach to watch large waves—this is extremely dangerous.
  • Secure boats and move vehicles away from the shoreline.
  • Know your barangay's designated evacuation center and route.
  • Return home only when local authorities declare it safe.
